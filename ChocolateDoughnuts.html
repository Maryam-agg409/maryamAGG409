Chocolate Doughnuts

These chocolate filled doughnuts are absolutely delicious for just any day, ESPECIALLY for a cold day. They are soft, warm, sweet and full of happiness.

Prep Time Cook Time Ready In Serves
30m 2hr 30m 3hr 12

Ingredients:

  • 50g caster sugar, plus 5- 6 heaped tbsp for coating the doughnuts
  • 150ml whole milk
  • 15g yeast
  • 50g unsalted butter
  • 320g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • Pinch of sea salt
  • 2 egg yolks
  • Vegetable oil enough for deep-frying
  • 250ml heavy cream
  • 2-4 tbsp honey
  • 250g chopped dark amd milk chocolate
  • 60g unsalted cold butter, cubed
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon

Direction:

    For the Dough

  1. Heat the sugar and milk in a pan for about 5 minutes until the milk is warm and the sugar has dissolved.
  2. Add yeast into a bowl and pour in half the warm milk. Mix and set side.
  3. Place the remaining milk back on the heat and add the butter. Heat on low flame for about 3 minutes (the milk must not boil).
  4. Sift the flour and salt into a large bowl. Add in the egg yolks, yeasted milk and the hot milk. Mix together to make a soft dough.
  5. Knead the dough with your hands onto a floured surface for a few minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic. (Dust with a little extra plain flour if the mix is too sticky).
  6. Place the dough in a clean bowl and cover it with cling film. Set aside for 1-2 hours until it doubles in size.

    7. For the Filling

  7. Pour the cream in a saucepan and mix in the honey to your liking.
  8. Put the chocolate and butter into a bowl. When the cream is almost boiling, pour it over the chocolate mixture and stir until well mixed, smooth and glossy.

    9. Final Steps

  9. Knead the risen dough again, lightly on a floured work surface. Roll out the dough to a thickness of about 2cm and shape it into a rectangle.
  10. Cut the dough into 12 smaller rectangles, and place them on a baking paper lined and leave to rise for 30-40 minutes until doubled in size, again. (This will make the doughnuts light and fluffy once cooked.)
  11. Combine the remaining sugar with the nutmeg and cinnamon powder in a bowl (this will be used to coat the doughnuts).
  12. Put the oil in a deep frying pan and heat the oil to 170Â°C.
  13. Fry the doughnuts, in batches, for 3-4 minutes until evenly golden brown and cooked through. (You will need to moderate the heat between batches according to whether the doughnuts brown too quickly or too slowly.)
  14. Using a slotted spoon, remove the doughnuts from the hot oil and roll in the nutmeg and cinnamon sugar to coat.
  15. Fill a piping bag with the warm chocolate and use a small nozzle to fill the doughnuts.
    16. Serve after 10 mins or after the chocolate doesn't run and becomes thick.
